# On the Snake Chain — London, 1840

## The Hook: The Undulating Cord
The Snake chain is a study in mechanical biomimicry. Known in the industry as the "Brazilian chain," it is a sleek, tubular masterpiece that moves with the uncanny, undulating fluidity of a serpent. It is the only chain that truly achieves the aesthetic of a solid gold cord—a continuous, mirror-finished cylinder that lacks the visible "breaks" of traditional link jewelry. It is designed for the purist, providing a frictionless surface that serves as the ultimate high-contrast anchor for a singular, exceptional pendant.

## The Origin & Design Evolution: A Victorian Obsession
The modern Snake chain’s lineage can be traced back to the mid-19th century. Its popularity was ignited in 1840, when Prince Albert presented Queen Victoria with an engagement ring in the shape of a serpent—a symbol of eternal, unbroken love. This royal endorsement sparked a "Serpent Mania" across the British Empire. Hand-crafted snake chains, once the exclusive province of master goldsmiths, were later mechanized in the late 1850s, allowing the "snakeskin" texture to be produced with the mathematical precision we recognize today.

## The Engineering: The Interlocked Plate
The secret to the Snake chain’s "liquid" movement is its internal architecture. It is not made of loops, but of small, slightly curved metal plates or bands. These plates are joined tightly together at an angle, interlocking to form a continuous, hollow tube. This "hinge-link" construction allows the chain to drape with a semi-rigid elegance, preventing it from tangling or snagging on the finest fabrics or hair. Because there are no open loops, the surface area for light reflection is maximized, resulting in a consistent, radiant glow.

## Cultural Icons: The Minimalist’s Anchor
From the intricate "Ouroboros" necklaces of the Victorian era to the sleek, minimalist pendants of the 1990s, the Snake chain has been the quiet engine of high fashion. It is the "invisible" chain of choice for high-end jewelers, favored for its ability to support substantial weight without distracting from a centerpiece. To wear a 2mm Snake chain is to carry the weight of a solid gold rope with the visual subtlety of a silken thread.

## The Bullion Thread: The Solid Gold Cylinder
Despite its slender appearance, the Snake chain is deceptively dense. Because the plates are tightly packed with minimal air gaps, it carries significant gold weight per inch.
