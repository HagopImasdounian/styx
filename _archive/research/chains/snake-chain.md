# The Snake Chain: The Brazilian Fluidity

The Snake chain, often called the **Brazilian chain**, is a sleek, tubular design that mimics the smooth, undulating movement of a serpent. It is a masterpiece of mechanical interlocking, providing a continuous, mirror-like surface.

## 1. Historical Origin and Design Evolution
- **Ancient Inspiration:** While coiled jewelry existed in Ancient Egypt and Greece, the modern "snake chain" as a series of interlocked plates is a 19th-century invention.
- **Victorian Era (1840s):** Snake motifs were incredibly popular in Victorian England after Prince Albert gave Queen Victoria a snake-shaped engagement ring. Hand-crafted snake chains became a height-of-fashion status symbol.
- **Mechanization (1850s):** The transition to machine-made snake chains occurred in the late 1850s, with patents in the US (notably by Bryce Grey Nichol) allowing for mass production of this once-laborious design.

## 2. Design Rationale
- **"Snakeskin" Texture:** Unlike link chains made of wire loops, the snake chain is constructed from small, slightly curved metal plates or bands.
- **Tight Interlocking:** These plates are joined tightly together at an angle, creating a continuous tube. This prevents the chain from tangling or snagging on fine hair.
- **Semi-Rigid Drape:** The "hinge-like" connection between plates allows the chain to drape elegantly like a solid gold cord, though it cannot be knotted without kinking.

## 3. Fact vs. Conjecture
- **Fact:** The name "Brazilian chain" is a common industry synonym, likely due to a period where Brazilian manufacturers dominated the export of high-quality round snake chains.
- **Conjecture:** Some claim the chain was designed to emulate a real snake's scales for protection; historically, it was more likely a purely aesthetic attempt to create a "liquid" metal texture.

## 4. Fun Facts & Cultural References
- **Victorian Symbolism:** To the Victorians, the snake represented **eternal love** and the cycle of life (the Ouroboros), making snake chains popular anniversary gifts.
- **The "Invisible" Chain:** Because of its smooth surface, it is the preferred chain for high-end minimalist pendants, as it doesn't distract from the centerpiece.
- **Fragility Warning:** A snake chain is notoriously difficult to repair. If it "kinks," the internal plate structure is often compromised, leading to a permanent dent.

## 5. Technical Thread: Typical Grams per Inch
Snake chains are denser than they appear because they are essentially a solid tube of metal plates.

| Width (mm) | 10k Gold (approx. g/in) | 14k Gold (approx. g/in) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0.8mm** | 0.12 – 0.14g | 0.15 – 0.18g |
| **1.0mm** | 0.35 – 0.40g | 0.40 – 0.45g |
| **1.5mm** | 0.55 – 0.60g | 0.65 – 0.75g |
