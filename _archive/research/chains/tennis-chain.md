# The Tennis Chain: The Eternity Line Reborn

The Tennis chain is a symbol of athletic elegance and modern luxury. Known for its seamless line of identical gemstones, its name is born from one of the most famous mishaps in sports history.

## 1. Historical Origin & Popularization
- **Origin:** Before 1978, this style was known as an **"eternity line"** or **"rivière"** necklace. 
- **The Chris Evert Incident:** During the **1978 U.S. Open**, tennis legend Chris Evert's diamond bracelet snapped and flew off her wrist during a match. She requested a pause in the match to find her diamonds on the court. 
- **Popularization:** In the post-match interview, she referred to it as her "tennis bracelet," and the name immediately stuck. The "tennis chain" followed as the necklace equivalent of this design.

## 2. Design Rationale
- **Seamless Line:** The design features an uninterrupted row of gemstones set in individual gold or platinum baskets.
- **Flexibility:** Unlike rigid necklaces, the tennis chain is engineered to be highly flexible, allowing it to move smoothly with the wearer—a nod to its "sporty" heritage.
- **The Safety Clasp:** Since the Evert incident, high-quality tennis chains almost always feature a **double-locking safety clasp** to prevent the piece from being lost during activity.

## 3. Fact-Checking
- **Verified History:** The Chris Evert incident is a well-documented televised event. However, for years it was misreported as happening in 1987. Evert corrected the record in 2022, confirming it was actually **1978**.
- **Conjecture:** Some claim the style was specifically designed for tennis players. This is false; the style (Eternity Line) had existed for decades as high-jewelry evening wear before it became associated with the sport.

## 4. Fun Facts & Cultural References
- **Serena Williams:** Continuing the legacy, Serena frequently wears multi-million dollar "real" tennis chains during her grand slam matches.
- **The "Rivière":** In French, the style is called *rivière* (river), describing how the diamonds "flow" around the neck like water.
- **Zendaya & Tennis-Core:** During the press tour for the film *Challengers*, Zendaya's use of tennis jewelry sparked a 130% increase in global search interest for the style.

## 5. Technical Thread: Gold Density
Tennis chains are unique because their weight is shared between the gold "baskets" and the gemstones. For the "bullion" premise, we focus on the gold mounting weight.

| Width | 10k Gold (approx. g/in) | 14k Gold (approx. g/in) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **3mm** | 1.10 – 1.30g | 1.30 – 1.50g |
| **5mm** | 1.80 – 2.10g | 2.20 – 2.50g |

*Note: These weights are for the gold mounting only. The total weight will increase based on the carat weight of the gemstones (diamonds or alternatives) used.*
