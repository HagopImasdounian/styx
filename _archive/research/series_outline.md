# Styx Journal: "Bullion You Can Wear" Series Outline

## Core Philosophy
The objective of this series is to educate customers on the intrinsic value of gold jewelry. We move beyond "fashion" to frame each chain as a portable, wearable asset. Every article must reinforce the transparency of gold weight (grams per inch) and the historical durability of the design.

## Series Structure
The articles will be grouped into the five categories provided, allowing for a structured "Lookbook" of information.

### I. The Powerhouses (Popular & Bold)
*Focus: Cultural dominance and massive gold volume.*
- **Cuban Link:** The Miami powerhouse.
- **Figaro Chain:** The Italian classic.
- **Franco Chain:** The indestructible weave.
- **Curb Chain:** The equestrian roots of the heavy link.
- **Mariner Chain:** Nautical strength and the "Gucci" legacy.

### II. The Artisans (Woven & Braided)
*Focus: Technical complexity and high surface area.*
- **Rope Chain:** Ancient Egyptian roots and 80s hip-hop "Dookie" chains.
- **Wheat Chain (Spiga):** Nature-inspired symmetry and pendant strength.
- **Criss-Cross / Tinsel / Forsantina:** Modern engineering for maximum light reflection.

### III. The Foundations (Round & Rolling)
*Focus: Timelessness and versatility.*
- **Cable Chain:** The "DNA" of jewelry.
- **Rolo Chain:** Boxing heritage (Jem Belcher) and Victorian elegance.
- **Ball / Singapore:** From military dog tags to Italian twisted hybrids.

### IV. The Architects (Flat & Architectural)
*Focus: Geometry and "Liquid Gold" flow.*
- **Herringbone:** The 80s power accessory and Egyptian "flat-wire" legacy.
- **Snake Chain:** Interlocking plates and the Victorian "serpent" trend.
- **Box / Paperclip:** Venetian craftsmanship and modern industrial symbolism.

### V. The Ornamentals (Figural & Decorative)
*Focus: Artistic expression and specialized links.*
- **Tennis Chain:** The 1978 Chris Evert incident and the evolution of the "line" bracelet.
- **Valentino / Tulip / Heart:** Blending high-fashion geometry with floral/sentimental motifs.

## Article Template (The Storytelling Framework)
To ensure consistency and "citability" for GEO (Generative Engine Optimization), each article will follow this flow:

1.  **The Hook:** A bold statement about the chain's presence (e.g., "The Cuban link isn't just a chain; it's a 30-gram statement of liquid equity.")
2.  **The Origin Myth vs. The Truth:** Fact-checking the history (Ancient Egypt vs. 1970s Miami).
3.  **The Engineering:** Why it’s designed this way (e.g., the Franco's V-weave to prevent kinking).
4.  **Cultural Icons:** Who wore it first? Who wore it best? (Run-DMC, Jay-Z, Serena Williams).
5.  **The Investment Thread (The "Gold Ticker"):** A breakdown of the gold-per-inch ratio.
    - Why 10k/14k is the "Sweet Spot" for durability vs. purity.
    - Calculation of "Wearable Bullion" value.
